A real walk-through

Watch it work, start to finish.

Testing northwind.com — a payroll app for small teams. Here's exactly what happens, and what comes back at each step.

1 Point One line. That's the entire input.

No setup, no script. One command kicks the whole thing off.

generate for northwind.com
2 Generate It reads the site, then casts the buyers.

Claude opens the homepage, pricing, and nav — then writes four buyers and five tasks from what it actually saw. Here's one:

personas / maya-skeptic.md

Maya Okonkwo — office manager at a 22-person design studio. Switched payroll tools twice; both migrations were painful. Reads the price before anything else, refuses to sit through a sales demo to find out what something costs.

says "just show me the price" · avoids "book a demo"

tasks / — 5 generated
  • 01 · First impression
  • 02 · Find the right plan
  • 03 · What does it cost? ← running this one
  • 04 · Can I trust them?
  • 05 · Start a free trial
3 Run It becomes Maya and clicks through, out loud.

One command. Claude opens a real browser as Maya, navigates the site, and narrates what she's thinking — in her words. This is the moment she hits the pricing page:

run the skeptic through task 03
sessions / maya / 03 / transcript.md
MMaya · Task 03 — What does it cost? PARTIAL
"I'm not booking a demo just to find out what this costs. If the price is hidden behind a form, that already tells me how this relationship is going to go."

5 Synthesize Every session rolls up into one report.

Run all four buyers, then ask for the summary. Claude reads every transcript and writes findings ranked by severity — each backed by the quote that proves it.

npm run synthesize

Pricing is hidden behind a demo form — no number appears anywhere on the site. 3 of 4 buyers stalled here, and the skeptic left without converting.

A closer look · Playwright

How step 3 is even possible.

Playwright gives Claude hands and eyes — it opens a real Chromium browser, clicks and types like a person, then feeds the screen back as screenshots. That loop repeats on every single action:

DecideClaude, in character
→
ActPlaywright clicks & types
→
Observescreenshot + page back
↺

Real browser, real clicks — which is what makes the efficiency score honest.

A closer look · the scores

What those five numbers mean.

Task success Did they actually finish? Pass · Partial · Fail
Efficiency How direct was the path? ideal steps ÷ real steps
Ease — SEQ "How easy was that?" 1 – 7
Intent "Would you take the next step?" 1 – 7
Confidence "Are you sure you found it?" 1 – 7
SessionScore One number to rank every session 0 – 1 composite

When confidence is high but success is low, the gap flags a false success — a screen that fools people into thinking they're done.

What it's made of

Four layers. No backend.

Claude Code reasoning · role-play · orchestration the brain
Playwright clicks, types & navigates a real browser the hands
Markdown instructions CLAUDE.md + prompts that shape behaviour the rules
Node scripts synthesis + automated quality checks the checks

The craft lives in those middle two layers — if you can write instructions in plain English, you can rebuild this.

The obvious objection

"But AI just makes things up."

The kit doesn't trust its own word. Every transcript runs through automated checks before a finding counts:

✓ Every metric present & math checks out ✓ Persona stayed in their own vocabulary ✓ Factual claims flagged to verify vs. screenshots ✓ No more steps than the task allowed ✓ No contradictions ("failed" but rated 7/7)
